Section 7 — Niche Positioning

Find Wide's Niche and Own It

Whimsy's two niches — hazmat and tri-axle/overweight — command 20–35% rate premiums and attract shippers with almost no price sensitivity. Wide needs to pick one lane and go deep.

☣️
Option A: Hazmat Certification
Get 10–15 drivers hazmat-certified. This unlocks chemical, industrial, and energy shipper contracts. Very few local drayage carriers can handle hazmat containers legally. Rate premiums are significant and shipper loyalty is extremely high — they don't switch once they find a reliable hazmat carrier.
Cost: ~$200–400 per driver for certification
🏗️
Option B: Overweight / Tri-Axle
Lease or purchase tri-axle chassis. Overweight containers from ports and rail facilities need specialized equipment to move legally. The Chicago market has significant overweight container volume from steel, heavy equipment, and bulk commodity imports. Wide's South Holland location is ideally placed to serve this.
Cost: $800–1,200/mo per chassis
❄️
Option C: Temperature-Controlled
Reefer container drayage from rail ramps to food distribution centers. Chicago is a major food import hub — Jewel-Osco, US Foods, Sysco, Gordon Food Service all receive refrigerated containers at Chicago ramps. A small reefer-capable fleet commands premium rates and builds long-term accounts with food distribution vendors.
Cost: Reefer units add ~$500–800/mo per truck in operating cost

Pick one niche in the first 12 months. Market it on the website. Put it in the company description on FMCSA, IANA, and load board profiles. Build a reputation before adding the second niche.

Section 8 — Technology

The Tech Stack That Supports Growth

🗺️
Transportation Management System (TMS)
Whimsy uses Trinium TMS (now CargoWise Landside). It powers their customer login portal, EDI integrations, load tracking, and invoicing. Wide should look at Rose Rocket, Turvo, or AxleHire — all are designed for mid-size drayage carriers with shipper portal functionality. A TMS is what separates a carrier from a trucking company in the eyes of enterprise shippers.
Cost: $500–2,000/mo depending on platform and fleet size
📡
EDI Integration
Electronic Data Interchange lets a shipper's ERP or WMS system talk directly to Wide's TMS — sending load tenders, receiving status updates, and processing invoices automatically. Once a shipper is EDI-connected, they cannot switch carriers without a full IT project. This is the ultimate stickiness tool.
Cost: $1,500–5,000 per shipper integration setup
📱
ELD & Driver App
Samsara is the industry standard. It handles ELD compliance, dash cam footage, GPS tracking, and can feed real-time location data into a shipper-facing tracking portal. Shippers increasingly require proof of ELD compliance and GPS tracking as part of carrier qualification. Samsara also reduces insurance premiums significantly over time.
Cost: ~$25–45/truck/mo for Samsara
🤖
Automated Load Confirmation & Invoicing
Once a TMS is in place, load confirmations, detention clock triggers, accessorial charges, and invoices can all be automated. This removes 5–10 hours of admin work per week at the current fleet size — and scales without adding headcount as the fleet grows. Less manual work also means fewer billing errors and faster payment cycles.
Cost: Included in most TMS platforms
Section 9 — What Makes Business Sticky

Four Retention Drivers That Lock In Revenue

🔐
1. TMS Customer Portal
Once a shipper's logistics team logs into Wide's portal daily to check container status, pulling that workflow out and rebuilding it with a new carrier is a 60–90 day IT project nobody wants to do. The portal turns a business relationship into an operational dependency. Churn drops significantly after adoption — typically 60%+ reduction in account loss.
This is the highest-value investment Wide can make for retention
🤝
2. Dedicated Lane Contracts
Spot loads are transactional — a shipper can use anyone on any given day. Dedicated lane contracts (even 90-day commitments) lock in volume, give drivers predictable schedules, and give Wide predictable revenue. The goal is to convert every repeat shipper from spot to dedicated within 6 months of their first load.
Preferred carrier status requires 50+ truck fleet
🔗
3. EDI / API Integration
When Wide's TMS receives load tenders and sends status updates automatically through EDI, the shipper's procurement team stops thinking about drayage as something they need to manage. Wide becomes infrastructure. Switching means rebuilding that integration — which nobody does unless there's a major service failure.
Setup cost: $1,500–5,000 per shipper integration
⭐
4. FMCSA Safety Score
Enterprise shippers and major 3PLs (C.H. Robinson, Echo, Coyote) run carrier safety audits before assigning loads. A clean CSA score is table stakes for these relationships. Whimsy's Carrier of the Year award from C.H. Robinson is directly connected to their safety record. Wide should treat their CSA score like a credit score — protect it at all costs.
A poor CSA score can disqualify Wide from entire shipper networks

Measuring Progress

The KPIs That Tell Wide If It's Working

🚛
Driver Count
Track total active drivers (company + OO) monthly. The progression that unlocks business: 35 drivers = higher shipper confidence. 50 drivers = preferred carrier eligibility. 75 drivers = Fortune 500 shortlist. 100+ drivers = dedicated contract negotiations open.
Target: 50+ drivers by month 12
📊
Driver Retention Rate
Monthly turnover in trucking averages 90%+ annualized industry-wide. A company with strong culture and transparent pay should target under 40% annual turnover. Track every driver who leaves — understand why. Exit interview data is gold for fixing retention problems before they compound.
Target: Under 40% annual turnover
📦
Dedicated Lane Contracts
Count the number of shippers on dedicated lane agreements vs. spot loads. The ratio matters more than total load count. A fleet with 80% dedicated volume is worth more, grows more predictably, and is far easier to manage operationally than a fleet chasing spot loads daily.
Target: 60% dedicated by month 12
🌐
Website Inbound Leads
Track monthly quote form submissions and driver applications from the website. Google Analytics will show where traffic comes from. Google Search Console shows which keywords Wide is ranking for. A properly SEO'd site should be generating 5–15 shipper inquiries per month within 6 months of launch.
Target: 10+ inbound leads/month by month 6
⭐
FMCSA CSA Score
Pull Wide's CSA score monthly at safer.fmcsa.dot.gov. The percentile scores across Safety Management, HOS Compliance, Vehicle Maintenance, and Driver Fitness determine whether enterprise shippers can even use Wide. Any score above 65th percentile in key categories triggers shipper disqualification at major 3PLs.
Target: All categories below 50th percentile
💰
Revenue Per Driver
As the fleet grows, track gross revenue divided by total drivers monthly. This number should increase as Wide moves from spot to dedicated volume and adds premium-rate specialty services (hazmat, overweight). Declining revenue per driver signals rate pressure — a sign to find higher-value freight or renegotiate lane rates.
Target: Increase 15–20% YoY as niche develops
