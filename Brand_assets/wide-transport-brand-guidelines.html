Brand Guidelines
v1.0 — 2025
Wide Transport is a Chicago-area intermodal drayage carrier based in South Holland, IL, operating within a 300-mile radius of Chicago and serving all major Midwest rail ramps. The brand communicates reliability, toughness, and precision — a company that shows up, moves freight, and doesn't complicate things. Every visual and verbal decision should reinforce that.

05 Voice & Tone
✓ Write This
"300-mile radius. 24/7 dispatch. No missed pickups."
Direct. Specific. Gives shippers what they actually need to know. Numbers over adjectives. Confidence without boasting.
✗ Not This
"A premier logistics partner committed to your success."
Vague, forgettable, sounds like every carrier. Wide's copy should always answer: what specifically, where, and how fast.
✓ Write This
"Home daily. No forced dispatch. Apply in 5 minutes."
Drivers make decisions fast. Speak to their real concerns: home time, autonomy, and how long applying takes. No fluff.
✗ Not This
"Join our family and grow your career with us!"
Drivers have heard this at every company. Instead, show it through specific policies, pay numbers, and real benefit details.
Confident
We state facts. We don't hedge. "We serve the Southland corridor" — not "we may be able to assist with your transportation needs."
Specific
Always use real numbers: service radius, ramps served, DOT number, pay details. Specificity builds trust faster than any adjective.
No Nonsense
Wide is not a tech company. The tone is industrial and honest. Skip corporate speak. Short sentences. Active voice. Get to the point.

08 Key Messages by Audience
For Shippers & Vendors
"300-mile reach. All major Chicago ramps. Quote in minutes."
Lead with speed and coverage. Shippers want to know: can you serve my lane? How fast can I get a rate? Give them those answers before anything else on the website.
→ Instant freight quote form on homepage
→ Credit application available to download
→ TMS portal login for existing accounts
→ FMCSA safety score referenced publicly
For Drivers & Owner Operators
"Home daily. No forced dispatch. Hazmat pay on top."
Drivers decide fast. If your pay and home-time policy aren't on the page in plain language, they move on. No vague promises — real numbers, real policies, real application link.
→ Pay details visible: hourly, OT threshold, bonuses
→ No forced dispatch — stated explicitly
→ Referral bonus dollar amount published
→ Online CDL application — apply in under 5 min
